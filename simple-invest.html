Simple Invest: Self-Serve Capabilities to Regional Markets

Mobile UX Design UX Research User Interface
ABOUT

THE CLIENT

UOB — one of Southeast Asia's leading banks — had already launched Simple Invest in Singapore as a self-serve wealth management product. The next phase: scale to Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia, each with distinct regulatory requirements, languages, and investment product availability.

THE PROJECT

Expand UOB's Simple Invest platform from Singapore to three additional regional markets. Design localized UI experiences, adapt product flows for country-specific regulations and user behaviours, and deliver a consistent yet contextually appropriate investment experience across all four markets.

PROCESS
01 Exploration & Discovery
▾
Audited the existing Singapore Simple Invest product to identify what was market-agnostic vs. Singapore-specific. Worked with regional product and legal teams to map regulatory constraints per country — understanding where UI flexibility existed and where flows were mandated.
02 Localization & Iteration
▾
Designed localized UI variants for each market — adapting language handling, layout density, risk disclosure flows, and product category presentation to local context. Maintained a shared component system to ensure design consistency while accommodating meaningful regional differences.
03 Cross-Regional Collaboration
▾
Coordinated with product managers, engineers, and legal representatives across 4 countries simultaneously. Maintained design alignment across markets while navigating different release timelines, compliance reviews, and local stakeholder feedback cycles — ensuring the Singapore baseline remained coherent with each localized variant.
KEY FEATURES
01
Self-Serve Investment Capabilities
End-to-end investment flow designed for non-advised, self-directed customers — from product discovery and risk profiling to order placement and portfolio tracking.
02
Localized UI Experiences
Country-specific UI adaptations covering language, regulatory disclosures, product availability, and cultural UI preferences — ensuring each market felt native, not adapted.
IMPACT

BUSINESS

  • Expanded UOB's wealth management reach to 3 new regional markets
  • Enabled self-serve customer acquisition without relationship manager overhead
  • Reduced time-to-market for regional launches through shared design system

DESIGN

  • Established scalable localization patterns reusable across future UOB products
  • Maintained visual and interaction consistency across all 4 country variants
  • Streamlined handoff with a single multi-market component library

TECHNOLOGY

  • Reduced engineering complexity through shared component architecture
  • Design specs aligned with existing UOB mobile engineering frameworks
  • Enabled parallel development across markets without design bottlenecks
KEY TAKEAWAYS

LOCALIZATION

  • Localisation is more than translation — layout, hierarchy, and metaphors differ by market
  • Early legal alignment saves significant late-stage redesign
  • Regional user research reveals unexpected behavioural differences

SCALABILITY

  • Invest in a shared component foundation before market-specific work begins
  • Atomic design thinking enables rapid variant generation with minimal drift
  • Documentation designed for handoff, not just internal reference

COLLABORATION

  • Multi-timezone stakeholder management requires proactive async communication
  • Trust is built through transparent decision logs, not just deliverables
  • Regional champions within each country team accelerate alignment
SUMMARY

Led the UX and UI expansion of UOB's Simple Invest platform from Singapore to Malaysia, Thailand, and Indonesia — delivering localized self-serve investment experiences tailored to each market's regulatory environment and user expectations. Designed a scalable multi-market system that reduced engineering overhead, maintained brand consistency, and gave UOB a repeatable playbook for future regional product launches.
